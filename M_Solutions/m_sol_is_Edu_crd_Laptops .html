Plan for a Full Life Span of the Device

Today’s increasingly technology-driven society requires students to develop useful tech skills, which are often cultivated through personalized education. In a 1:1 scenario, where every student has their own device, learning can match specific student needs. Those needs will inevitably change over time, which is why it is important to enable future flexibility in device performance to extend the life of your laptop investment.

For example, today a student may be accessing content and tomorrow be creating it. Even in early education, students are multitasking with productivity, collaboration, videoconferencing, and web-based tools running simultaneously. As they progress in school, add to that content creation, coding, 2D/3D modeling, and more-advanced applications—all requiring greater computing power as the student experience grows over time. Considering only the minimum specifications (specs) for a laptop device today could shortchange the device’s usability in the future.

By thinking ahead and selecting a model upgrade beyond the basic recommended specs, you enable the device to grow with the student while making a future-forward device investment for the next several years.

Features to Look For

In addition to performance needs, consider the following key features when it comes time to purchase a laptop for education:

  • Connectivity: Interruptions, slowdowns, or distractions caused when waiting for long load times keep students from getting the most out of their active learning time. Look for devices that have the right capabilities for reliable access to the internet and that can support new connection standards, like Wi-Fi 6 or Wi-Fi 6E (Gig+). Some Intel-based Windows OS devices may be eligible for the Intel® Connectivity Performance Suite, which optimizes Wi-Fi performance with up to 30 percent faster speeds by dynamically switching to the best access point connection.1
  • Compatibility: The ideal student laptop is compatible with the devices it will be used alongside, meaning it has the right ports and options to support equipment and USB peripherals such as headphones, monitors, interactive flat panel displays, or other devices. Laptops with Thunderbolt™ 4 ports can help to simplify connectivity to external devices and provide high-bandwidth connections and charging capabilities. Aligning your laptop choice with other education technology investments will also help maximize results across the board.
  • Ruggedness: Being highly portable, laptops must endure rough handling while being moved between learning environments. Durability features like spillproof keys, scratch-resistant displays, heavy-duty hinges, and round edges can help extend the life of the device and reduce downtime due to accidental drops and heavy use. Screen size can also have an impact on ease of portability as it contributes to overall device weight.
  • Long battery life: Students today are using their devices to learn and “do”, including assignments, projects, and creative exercises, in a range of environments. Additionally, processing-intensive videoconferencing, streaming, and advanced data and content creation applications require more power to operate seamlessly. Look for laptops, like Intel-based PCs and Chromebooks, that support all-day battery life and fast charging so students have the freedom to move between learning areas and complete work without needing to be tethered to an electrical outlet.

Education Laptop Device Security

Intel understands the importance of protecting students, faculty, and staff from cyber threats that can compromise their safety, security, and privacy. Cyberattacks today are increasing in sophistication, and schools are among the largest groups being targeted.2

Software-based security alone is no longer enough to combat threats, and a more comprehensive cybersecurity strategy in education that includes both hardware- and software-based security protection is required.

To provide the level of protection needed without making securing devices an arduous task, all Intel-based laptops come with a level of integrated hardware-based security technologies to help protect devices at and below the OS level.

Managing Education Laptops

For schools looking to support or currently supporting a 1:1 device model (one device per student or teacher), maintenance and management of devices becomes a time-consuming process for education IT teams. For large districts, rural districts with geographically dispersed locations, and schools supporting online learning—where students can participate in learning from a nonschool location—device management can become unmanageable, leading to delays in routine maintenance, troubleshooting, and issue resolution. These delays can hinder the teaching and learning experience and, ultimately, drive up IT costs as well as impact learning outcomes.

To address these challenges, schools can look for devices that support remote manageability, such as Intel vPro® platform-based devices that come with integrated, hardware-based manageability features. Intel® Active Management Technology (Intel® AMT) allows IT to remotely fix issues, repair corrupted drivers, and install software even when the operating system is down. Additionally, Intel® Endpoint Management Assistant (Intel® EMA) provides IT with a security-enabled, remote, hardware-based connection to manage devices inside and outside of a school’s firewall. This kind of robust remote management is also useful for interactive whiteboards, which are often used for active learning in classrooms.

Learning today happens in a range of environments both within and outside school facilities. Students who have laptop devices can access the technologies and tools they need as they choose from any environment to learn, develop, and hone the skills and mindsets for personal and future professional development.
