Business leaders are facing the formidable challenge of prioritizing the transition to net zero while keeping pace with the mainstreaming of processor-hungry technologies like AI and IoT. They are working towards two critical agendas: reducing their carbon footprint while simultaneously adopting ever more powerful technologies. Leadership must make significant headway towards becoming Tech Positive.

Read more about the Tech Positive Opportunity

CTOs not only have the vision and the appetite to accelerate sustainable transformation, they also have a mandate from the rest of the C-suite. As technology and sustainability converge, technology leaders must embrace their new role as the most pivotal sustainability influencer in the C-suite: the Sustainable CTO. This is a new model of tech leader who can deploy technology to build an organization that is fairer, greener, and smarter.

Read more about the Sustainable CTO

CTOs have the motivation to drive tech zero and tech positive. But they cannot do it alone. There are significant organizational barriers currently standing in their way. For CTOs to become successful Sustainable CTOs, they will need board-level support to help them overcome the knowledge, innovation, and investment barriers.

Learn more about the Tech Trilemma

The CTO’s Sustainability Roadmap

CTOs are under pressure to land tech zero and harness technology to effect the sustainable transformation of their organization and become tech positive. They are embracing their potential role as the greatest driver of sustainability in their organization, with 83% aspiring to become a Sustainable CTO. If business leaders can help CTOs overcome the tech trilemma, businesses can harness technology in new ways to achieve their organizational goals and have a positive impact on the world.

Discover the best practice for Sustainable CTOs

“Enterprise leaders are approaching the interconnected challenges of sustainability and radical digital transformation. Technology has a critical role to play in sustainable transformation. If the C-suite, including CTOs and CIOs, rally in support of a tech-positive approach, technological transformation can propel us toward a future that is greener, fairer and smarter.”

—Greg Lavender, senior vice president, chief technology officer and general manager of the Software and Advanced Technology Group
