Higher Ed Tech Shapes Our Future

Using the right technology can be pivotal for higher ed studies, research, and next-gen innovation. That said, it can be challenging to understand what is needed for higher education technology. As a technology leader and active education supporter, Intel works closely with education device decision-makers, lab and library technology purchasers, and educators to understand the specific needs of the education market. From those insights come the technologies that enable students and educators to innovate and shape our collective future.

Selecting the Right Device for Advanced Learning

When making a large-scale device purchase for your academic institution, you want confidence that your selection will support the diverse needs of your students, faculty, and IT staff, both today and in the future. And as a student buying a computer for school, you want to be sure you get the most out of your purchase with a device that can help you do your best work, whatever you may choose to study.

With a range of form factors and configurable options available to you, ensuring whatever device you select is based on a secure, proven, and stable platform is paramount. Here are three key capabilities to look for in any device you’re considering.

Built-in Security

Cyberattacks can target both software and firmware. Devices with integrated, hardware-enabled security features can help to reduce risks of attacks without adding IT infrastructure. For example, Intel vPro® platform-based devices with Intel® Hardware Shield provide hardware-to-software visibility and memory lockdown features to prevent malicious code injection. Additionally, Intel® Threat Detection Technology (Intel® TDT) delivers integrated deep defense protection at the application, data, and firmware levels.

Fast, Versatile Connectivity

Given the nature of higher education, students and educators need access to their systems from various locations and networks with reliability and performance. Look for devices with embedded wireless technologies and Thunderbolt™ 4 ports that allow dependable flexibility on the go.

Responsive Performance

From multitasking to video streaming to processing large data sets or graphics files, devices used in higher education need to support a range of applications and processing power needs without interrupting productivity. To get faster performance with fewer disruptions, select a device with a processor, such as Intel® Core™ processors, that can keep up with today’s demands and tomorrow’s needs.

How Much Performance Power Do You Need?

Processor requirements for a device vary based upon the way it will be used. To make an optimal investment and help future-proof your choice, consider both the needs of today and what will be needed in the coming years.
