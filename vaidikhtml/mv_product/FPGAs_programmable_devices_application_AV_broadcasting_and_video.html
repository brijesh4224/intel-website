Unlocking Broadcast and Video Production Advancements

From capture to consumption, digital video continues to evolve with new technologies and market trends. Increased resolutions and higher bit rates drive the need for a range of new connectivity standards, especially video over Internet Protocol (IP). The evolution to data center deployment models based on commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) hardware has realized advantages in flexibility, scalability, and cost.

The Intel portfolio can support and accelerate your entire workflow while enabling more flexibility for heterogeneous computing environments. We combine software and hardware programmability to help you meet the most demanding video processing and connectivity needs.

With core Intel technology building blocks, the same base platform architecture can meet processing requirements for your full end-to end-workflow including video production, postproduction, playout, distribution, and content delivery networks.

Enabling the End-to-End Broadcast Workflow

Bringing consumers their favorite streaming content or live events requires technologies across the video production workflow. Our portfolio and partners help make it possible at every step along the way.

Acquisition

Video acquisition needs to deliver the state-of-the-art image and audio quality to provide a stand-out consumer experience. Cameras and microphones are used for content capture and processing, while different connectivity solutions carry the content through the network to the video production equipment.

Video Production

Live video production is transitioning from its historic dependence on inflexible, costly proprietary standards and equipment to a more open model based on COTS hardware. Adopting an IP-based, software-defined deployment model for video production enables you to realize advantages in flexibility, scalability, and cost.

Video production includes essential content processing and manipulation technologies such as switching, mixing, color correction, replay operation, special effects, and graphics overlay. These operations must be performed in real time with nearly zero latency, demanding significant computational horsepower at high resolutions.

Video Distribution

Distribution allows content to be shared with the world. Final productions can be broadcast live or archived for later use. Video distribution typically involves encoding/transcoding to adjust the bit rate to available network bandwidth or storage capacity for archival.
