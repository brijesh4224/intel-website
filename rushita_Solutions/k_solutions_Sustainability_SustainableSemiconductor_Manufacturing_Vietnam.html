Water Restoration in Vietnam

To support Intel’s commitment to achieve net positive water, we have funded one water restoration project benefiting Vietnam. Once fully implemented, this project will restore an estimated 17 million gallons each year.

This page provides a summary of Intel-funded projects to support Vietnam water resources.

Rainwater Harvesting in Schools

Location: Saigon River Watershed, Vietnam

Implementation Partner:  CLEAN International

Estimated Restoration Benefit:  17.2 million gallons per year when fully implemented

Project Status:  Initiated in 2024

Project Description:  Rainwater harvesting can help mitigate seasonal water availability and quality risks in the Saigon River watershed by capturing excess runoff and using it to decrease the use of local groundwater supplies. Harvested rainwater can serve as a source of water for local schools in Vietnam, making it available for use throughout the schools, creating a more resilient and sustainable water supply. CLEAN International will implement rainwater harvesting systems at 86 schools in and around Hồ Chí Minh City over the course of 2024.

2030 Goal: Net Positive Water

Over the last two decades, our sustainable water management efforts have enabled us to conserve billions of gallons of water and return approximately 80% of our water use back to our communities. Now, we are broadening our focus to achieve net positive water use by conserving 60 billion gallons of water and funding water projects that restore more fresh water than we consume to our local watersheds.
