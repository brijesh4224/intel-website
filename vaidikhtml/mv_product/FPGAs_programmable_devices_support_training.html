Overview

Intel FPGA technical training offers many ways to learn. Sharpen your FPGA design skills today! All public training is free to attend.

Instructor-Led Classes

Learn FPGA design topics from expert instructors and earn badges for your achievements, all for FREE! Classes are taught as either one or a series of two half-day sessions. The virtual classroom allows you to attend from work or home. During class, access to a remote lab environment is provided – which means no setup is required to attend.

On-Demand eLearning

Learn at your convenience with our on-demand eLearning, all for FREE! The duration of each eLearning session typically ranges from approximately 30 minutes to one hour. A convenient player makes it easy to skip to the sections that interest you most.

Learning Plans

Access our learning plans by clicking on the provided links and logging in to the learning.intel.com website.

Beginner Intel® FPGA Designer

This learning plan is designed to familiarize individuals with a background in electronics, computer architecture, or related fields with the fundamentals of FPGAs, covering their history, structure, significance in the electronics industry, and enabling them to undertake their initial FPGA design.

Click to view the learning plan

Using Memory Interfaces in Intel Agilex® Devices

This comprehensive learning plan aims to prepare you with the essential knowledge and skills needed to effectively utilize Memory Interfaces in Intel Agilex® Devices, covering fundamental principles, practical applications, and debugging techniques.

Click to view the learning plan

Timing Analysis with the Intel® Quartus® Prime Pro Software

Participants in this comprehensive learning plan will explore FPGA design constraints and timing analysis using Intel® Quartus® Prime Pro Software, gaining a deep understanding of key factors in high-performance FPGA designs.

Click to view the learning plan

Using the Signal Tap Logic Analyzer

This learning plan aims to prepare you with the vital skills and expertise required to debug and resolve logic functional issues in FPGA designs using the Signal Tap Logic Analyzer, delving into both basic and advanced features of the tool.

Click to view the learning plan

Using the Nios® II Processor

This comprehensive learning plan aims to provide you with extensive expertise in utilizing the Nios II Processor through a series of eLearning courses covering hardware and software development, hardware abstraction layer, booting procedures, and customization of components and instructions.
