Overview

The Intel® oneAPI Technology Partner program is designed for you to play an important role in scaling oneAPI technical expertise to support new architectures, software applications, and services worldwide. Program members add value by combining their hardware and or software domain expertise with services (such as consulting, system integration, software application development and maintenance, and training) to meet the business and technical needs of their customers and the developer community.

Benefits

Technology and Knowledge Transfer

  • Regular advanced training on existing and upcoming products and technologies by Intel experts
  • Early access to technology samples when available
  • Access to course-specific Intel oneAPI instructional materials and resources

Co-marketing

  • Access to exclusive content to create and deliver campaigns, joint marketing programs, and events

Business Development

Requirements to Become a Partner

Join the Intel oneAPI Technology Partner program to get access to experts from Intel, resources, training, and business developments.

  • Must be a software company or service, technology consulting, or training company that provides software solutions with competencies and requirements, such as parallel programming for CPUs or GPUs, AI and machine learning, computer vision, natural language processing (NLP), and tuning of learning algorithms
  • Provide consulting and services, such as porting applications, coding, tuning, modernizing code, and training
  • Have an established confidential non-disclosure agreement (CNDA) with Intel or are willing to apply for one
  • Commit to taking Intel oneAPI training curriculum (16 to 17 courses and about 12 hours) and pass technical assessments with scores of 80% or higher
  • Maintain technology partner status through meeting program commitments and staying informed of oneAPI product updates

Application Process

The application process includes review and approval by the Intel oneAPI Technology Partner program team, based on the previous requirements.

If your application is approved, you then need to sign the Trademark License Agreement and Promotional Material Release.
